Somnath

The Somnath Temple, located in Gujarat, India, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and holds immense religious significance. It is believed to have been originally built by the Moon God, Chandra, and has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times throughout history. The present structure was reconstructed in 1951 under the leadership of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. It is a major pilgrimage site and attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Sun Temple

The Sun Temple in Modhera, Gujarat, is a magnificent 11th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God, Surya. Built by King Bhima I of the Chalukya dynasty in 1026 AD, it is an architectural masterpiece known for its intricate carvings and grand design. The temple complex includes the main shrine (Garbhagriha), an assembly hall (Sabha Mandap), and a sacred stepwell (Surya Kund) with over 100 small shrines.

Gir National Park

Gir National Park, located in Gujarat, India, is the only home of the Asiatic lions. Established in 1965, it covers around 1,412 square kilometers of dry deciduous forests, grasslands, and rocky hills. The park is rich in biodiversity, housing leopards, hyenas, sambar deer, and over 300 bird species. The best time to visit is from December to March, with jeep safaris offering wildlife sightings. Conservation efforts have helped increase the lion population significantly.
